The feet-together drill is a simple range exercise recommended by GOLF Top 100 teachers for developing balance and synchronizing arm swing with body rotation. Stand with your feet touching and hit balls with a 6-, 7-, or 8-iron at reduced speed. The narrow base forces your core to stabilize the swing, eliminating the sway and lunge patterns that lead to errant shots.

Once you're hitting solid contact with irons, extend the drill to a mid-iron tee shot: feet together, smooth tempo, and hold your finish until the ball lands. If you can maintain balance through impact with your feet touching, your normal-width stance will feel more stable and your weight transfer will be more efficient.

Top 100 teacher Kellie Stenzel recommends doing this at the start of a practice session rather than the end — the enforced efficiency carries over to the rest of your range work. Read more at [GOLF.com](https://golf.com/instruction/driving/golf-drill-find-fairways/).
