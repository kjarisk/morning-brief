Anthropic has announced a deal to take over all compute capacity at SpaceX's Colossus 1 data center in Memphis, Tennessee — access to more than 220,000 Nvidia GPUs including H100, H200, and the newest GB200 accelerators, representing over 300 megawatts of capacity. The announcement came alongside a separate CNBC interview in which CEO Dario Amodei disclosed that the company grew 80-fold in Q1 on an annualized basis rather than the 10-fold growth it had planned for, directly explaining the compute shortages users have experienced.

Amodei described the mismatch as unavoidable given the pace of demand: "That is the reason we have had difficulties with compute." The SpaceX partnership is a notable piece of the solution — and a surprising one, given that Elon Musk has been publicly critical of Anthropic. Musk said he spent time with senior Anthropic staff and decided to proceed after satisfying himself that "Claude is good for humanity." The companies have also expressed interest in eventually developing multiple gigawatts of compute capacity in orbit.

The scale of the deal signals that Anthropic is moving from GPU scarcity to GPU surplus, which should ease the rate-limit and availability complaints that have accompanied Claude's rapid enterprise growth. It also puts Anthropic's infrastructure footprint in a different category from where it was even three months ago.
