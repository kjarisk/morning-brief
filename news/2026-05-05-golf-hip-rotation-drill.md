Early extension — sliding the hips toward the ball instead of rotating through impact — is one of the most common swing faults and a leading cause of inconsistent ball-striking and loss of distance. This drill from Golf Monthly addresses the fault directly by giving golfers a clear physical reference for what correct hip rotation feels like, making it easy to self-diagnose and correct without a coach present.

The drill works by threading an alignment stick horizontally through your belt loops at address, then making practice swings focused entirely on rotating your lead hip back and away from the ball on the downswing. The belt buckle should face the target — not the ball — at impact. If the alignment stick tips toward the ball, early extension is happening. Ten short-iron swings with this focus builds the pattern faster than video analysis alone.

Golfers who successfully eliminate early extension typically report a noticeable increase in ball speed and a more consistent low point, which improves both distance and turf interaction with irons. The drill transfers well from the range to the course because the feeling of lead-hip clearance is distinctive and easy to recall mid-round.
