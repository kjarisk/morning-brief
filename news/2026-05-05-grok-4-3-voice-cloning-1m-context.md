xAI released Grok 4.3 with a sweeping set of capability upgrades and aggressive pricing. The model expands the context window to 1 million tokens, adds native video input, and introduces a full speech suite: a Text-to-Speech API priced at $4.20 per million characters, and a Custom Voices feature that clones a user's voice from one minute of audio. Speech-to-text supports 25 languages with both batch and streaming modes. Input pricing was cut 40% to $1.25 per million tokens, making Grok 4.3 one of the cheapest frontier-class models on the market.

The release carries over the 16-agent Heavy system and 2-million-token context from Grok 4.20 and is available immediately to SuperGrok Heavy tier subscribers. Full public rollout is expected in mid-to-late May. The voice cloning feature includes a two-stage passphrase and speaker-embedding consent gate, a notable addition given the regulatory attention synthetic voice generation has attracted in several jurisdictions.

For developers, the 1M-token context window and low per-token cost position Grok 4.3 as a competitive option for long-document analysis and agentic pipelines. The bundled speech APIs lower the barrier to building voice-first AI applications without additional third-party integrations.
