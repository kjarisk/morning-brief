DeepSeek released V4 on April 24, 2026, in two variants: V4 Pro (1.6 trillion total parameters, 49B active) and V4 Flash (284B total, 13B active). Both ship with a 1M-token context window and MIT licensing. V4 Pro tops LiveCodeBench at 93.5 and sits at Codeforces ELO 3206 — just ahead of GPT-5.5 at 3168 — and is statistically tied with Claude Opus 4.7 on SWE-bench Verified (80.6 vs 80.8). The API price: $1.74 per million input tokens, roughly one-seventh the cost of Claude Opus 4.7.

For developers, this resets the cost-performance frontier. Workloads that were budget-constrained when using frontier models can now run at near-frontier quality for everyday coding, retrieval, and reasoning tasks. The model is open-weights with MIT licensing, meaning it can also be self-hosted.

GPT-5.5 retains an advantage on Terminal-Bench 2.0 (complex command-line workflows: 82.7% vs 67.9%), so V4 Pro is not a universal replacement — but for most coding and reasoning tasks, the price gap is hard to justify. NIST's CAISI team published an independent evaluation in May 2026.
