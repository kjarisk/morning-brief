Canadian enterprise AI company Cohere announced it is acquiring Heidelberg-based Aleph Alpha in a deal valuing the combined entity at $20 billion. Schwarz Group — the German retail and tech conglomerate behind Lidl and Kaufland — will serve as lead investor in a new Series E, committing €500 million in structured financing. Both the Canadian and German governments attended the Berlin announcement and backed the deal under the Canada-Germany Sovereign Technology Alliance signed earlier in 2026.

The strategic rationale is explicit: the two companies want to offer enterprises and governments a credible alternative to routing sensitive data through US-based hyperscalers like Microsoft Azure or Google Cloud. Aleph Alpha brings years of EU AI Act compliance experience, deep government and public-sector relationships in German-speaking Europe, and a proven track record in regulated industries including defence, healthcare, and finance. Cohere brings scale, enterprise go-to-market capability, and a broader product portfolio.

The deal creates immediate pressure on Mistral AI, whose €13.7 billion valuation now trails the combined entity. Analysts expect Mistral to respond with a major capital raise or strategic partnership before the end of 2026.
