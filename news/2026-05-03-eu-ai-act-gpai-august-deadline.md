August 2, 2026 is when the EU AI Office gains full enforcement power over general-purpose AI (GPAI) models — including the ability to impose fines of up to 3% of global annual turnover for violations. European GPAI providers like Mistral AI will be directly subject to European Commission oversight, rather than national regulators, since GPAI enforcement is centralized at the EU level.

With 90 days to go, providers must ensure they have documented their model capabilities and systemic risks, implemented technical and governance measures from the GPAI Code of Practice, and are ready for Commission requests for information or model access. The AI Office can also order model recalls in extreme cases.

The practical pinch is for European startups: they face the same obligations as US hyperscalers but without equivalent legal and compliance infrastructure. Mistral AI is frequently cited as the key test case for how a European frontier GPAI provider navigates the new rules.
