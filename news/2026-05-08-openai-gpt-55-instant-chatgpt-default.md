OpenAI quietly swapped ChatGPT's default model from GPT-5.3 Instant to GPT-5.5 Instant on May 5, 2026, rolling the change out to all users. The key headline number: 52.5% fewer hallucinated claims on high-stakes prompts (medicine, law, finance) compared to the previous default. Responses are also shorter and less padded — 30% fewer words, 29% fewer lines — while keeping an appropriate tone.

The other meaningful change is personalization. For Plus and Pro users, GPT-5.5 Instant now draws on past chats, uploaded files, and connected Gmail to tailor responses. A new "memory sources" panel shows exactly which personal context was used in any given reply, and users can delete or correct individual entries. The intent is to make the model feel less generic without requiring users to re-explain their situation on every new chat.

In the API, GPT-5.5 Instant is available as `chat-latest`. OpenAI plans to roll the personalization features to Free, Go, Business, and Enterprise tiers in the coming weeks. The model doesn't replace the o-series reasoning models — it's the everyday default, not the hard-thinking option.
