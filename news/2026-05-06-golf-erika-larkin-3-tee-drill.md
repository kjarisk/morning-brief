Golf Digest 50 Best Teacher Erika Larkin recommends controlling the low point of your swing as the single most important skill for consistent iron play. Her 3-Tee Drill makes this concrete: tee up three balls in a row, the first on a high tee, the second on a medium tee, and the third flush to the ground. For each ball, hover the clubhead at that height before starting your swing, forcing your brain to find the correct arc for each contact point.

The drill builds the proprioceptive awareness needed to bottom out the swing at or just past the ball — not before it. Working through all three heights in sequence exposes any tendency to sweep (good on the high tee but not the low) or dig (necessary on the low but a mishit on the high). Done consistently, it builds a repeatable low point that produces predictable distance and direction from the turf.
