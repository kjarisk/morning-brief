GOLF Top 100 Teacher Tommy O'Brien Jr. explains a counterintuitive fix for golfers who chunk or blade bunker shots: most instruction has told players to "hit the sand behind the ball," but that cue actually encourages a low point that stays too far back, producing both heavy shots and skulled ones. The fix is to stop thinking about hitting behind the line and instead train yourself to enter the sand right at or just forward of the ball's position.

The drill is simple: draw a line in the sand directly behind the ball before each practice shot. The goal is not to enter the sand behind that line — the goal is to enter AT or slightly ahead of it. This retrains the bottom of your arc to be in the right place without requiring any conscious mechanical overhaul of your swing.

O'Brien notes that this also helps with consistency: if you're always trying to hit behind the ball, small timing errors produce dramatic mis-hits, whereas training a forward low point gives you more margin for error on both sides.
