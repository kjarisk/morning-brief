Anthropic previewed Claude Mythos on April 7, 2026, a frontier model so capable at offensive security that the company decided not to release it publicly. In internal testing, Mythos identified thousands of previously unknown zero-day vulnerabilities across every major operating system and browser. Given network access, it could execute multi-stage attacks autonomously — work that would take human professionals days.

Rather than a public release, Anthropic launched Project Glasswing: a controlled program where 12 partner organizations (AWS, Apple, Cisco, CrowdStrike, Google, JPMorgan Chase, Linux Foundation, Microsoft, Nvidia, and Palo Alto Networks) use Mythos Preview exclusively for defensive security work — finding and patching critical software before attackers can exploit it. Over 99% of the vulnerabilities found have not yet been patched, so details are being withheld under coordinated disclosure.

The model's existence was first revealed via a data leak in March 2026, with Anthropic confirming it represented a "step change" in capabilities. This is one of the clearest demonstrations to date that AI has crossed into territory where certain models may be too dangerous to deploy without tightly controlled access.
