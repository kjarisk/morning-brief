On April 24, 2026, Alphabet announced a commitment to invest up to $40 billion in Anthropic — $10 billion upfront in cash at a $350 billion valuation, with up to $30 billion more contingent on Anthropic hitting unspecified performance targets. Google Cloud also agreed to deliver five gigawatts of compute to Anthropic over five years.

The deal comes just days after Amazon announced an additional $5 billion investment in Anthropic (April 20). Combined, the two cloud giants have now committed over $45 billion to a single AI lab, while simultaneously competing with it via their own AI products (Gemini, Nova). The arrangement reflects how the hyperscalers see Anthropic as both a strategic tenant and a hedge against their own internal AI development falling short.

At $350 billion, Anthropic is now valued comparably to some of the largest traditional software companies. The practical effect of the Google commitment is that Anthropic gains access to enormous compute at preferential terms, which is increasingly the binding constraint on frontier model development.
