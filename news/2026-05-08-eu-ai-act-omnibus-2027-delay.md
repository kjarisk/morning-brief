The EU Council and European Parliament struck a provisional political deal on May 7, 2026 — after nine hours of negotiations — to significantly slim down and delay the AI Act's obligations. The headline change: compliance deadlines for stand-alone high-risk AI systems move from August 2, 2026 to December 2, 2027, and for high-risk AI embedded in products the new date is August 2, 2028. Machinery is now fully excluded from the AI Act's scope, since it already falls under separate sectoral rules.

The deal also clarifies supervision lines between the EU AI Office and national authorities, meaning law enforcement, financial institutions, and judicial authorities remain under member-state oversight. Positively, the co-legislators added one new provision: a ban on AI systems that generate non-consensual sexual or intimate imagery, or CSAM. Watermarking requirements for AI-generated content were preserved but given a deadline of December 2, 2026.

The rollback was explicitly driven by industry lobbying — European CEOs including Mistral, ASML, Airbus, Nokia, and SAP warned that "Europe is still debating regulation while others have shifted focus to scaling AI." The agreement is provisional and still requires formal ratification from both the Parliament and the Council, expected in the coming months.
