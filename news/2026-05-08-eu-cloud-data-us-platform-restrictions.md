European Commission officials are in discussions about requiring that financial, judicial, and health data processed by governments and public-sector bodies be hosted on sovereign cloud infrastructure rather than US hyperscaler platforms, CNBC reported on May 7, 2026. The proposal would be part of the EU's broader "Tech Sovereignty Package" — currently expected on May 27 — which also includes a Cloud and AI Development Act and Chips Act 2.0.

The cloud data restrictions, if adopted, would apply only to governments and public-sector organizations, not to private companies. The intent is to give the EU legally enforceable standards in line with its existing SEAL certification framework for cloud providers, which would move from voluntary to binding under the proposed Cloud and AI Development Act.

This sits alongside the EU AI Act rollback announced the same day. Together, the two signals point in opposite directions: the AI Act's compliance burden is being reduced to ease industry competition, while cloud data sovereignty rules would add new obligations for governments seeking to procure AI services from US providers. The Tech Sovereignty Package still needs Commission sign-off and ratification by all 27 member states before taking effect.
