Anthropic announced a research preview called "Dreaming" for Claude Managed Agents at its Code with Claude conference on May 6. The feature runs a scheduled process — typically overnight — in which agents review past work sessions, identify patterns they missed while performing tasks, and update their long-term memory stores. The goal is to let agents compound on experience: recurring mistakes get flagged, workflows that converge across users get reinforced, and team preferences get captured automatically.

Developers can configure dreaming for either fully automatic updates or manual review before memory changes are applied, giving teams control over how much autonomy they grant to the self-improvement loop. The feature works alongside Outcomes and multi-agent orchestration — both of which moved from research preview to public beta at the same event.

In practical terms this means a Claude agent handling code review or customer support could, over days and weeks, become materially better at your specific codebase or your team's style without any explicit prompt engineering. Access to the dreaming research preview requires a request to Anthropic.
