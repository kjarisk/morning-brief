Boris Cherny, head of Claude Code at Anthropic, joined Sequoia partner Lauren Reeder at AI Ascent 2026 to make a direct claim: coding is effectively solved for most of the work he does. Cherny says he has not written a line of code by hand since November 2025, yet remains one of the most productive engineers at Anthropic, shipping dozens of pull requests per day from his phone. The conversation covers what that shift actually looks like in practice, what kinds of problems Claude Code still fails at, and what he thinks developers should be learning instead of syntax.

The video was uploaded approximately three days ago and has become widely shared in developer circles. Cherny's framing is deliberately provocative — "coding is solved" is a testable claim, not a prediction — and the interview is notable for being concrete about failures as well as capabilities.

For developers using or evaluating Claude Code, this is the clearest account yet of how its creator actually uses the tool, including the workflows and the failure modes.
