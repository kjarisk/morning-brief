The European Commission published the second draft of its Code of Practice on Marking and Labelling of AI-generated content on May 3, 2026. The code guides providers and deployers of generative AI on how to comply with the EU AI Act's transparency obligations — specifically, how to mark AI-generated images, audio (including deepfakes), and text so that users can identify artificial content.

The code is framed as voluntary for now, but the underlying transparency obligations it implements become enforceable in August 2026. Compliance with the Code will be treated as a "safe harbour" demonstration that a provider meets the Act's requirements. Stakeholder feedback on this draft is open until June 2026, with a final version expected before enforcement begins.

For AI product teams serving European users, this is a practical compliance document worth reviewing now. The marking requirements will apply to consumer-facing generative AI features, and the August 2026 enforcement date is approaching.
