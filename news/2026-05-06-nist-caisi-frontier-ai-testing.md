The Center for AI Standards and Innovation (CAISI) at the US Department of Commerce announced new pre-deployment testing agreements with Google DeepMind, Microsoft, and xAI. Under the deals, CAISI will receive access to models — including versions with reduced safeguards — before public release, conducting national security capability evaluations and post-deployment research. CAISI has already completed more than 40 model evaluations since its first agreements with OpenAI and Anthropic in 2024.

The expansion follows the alarm raised by Anthropic's Mythos cybersecurity research, which disclosed that advanced AI models can discover and weaponise thousands of software vulnerabilities. The White House subsequently directed the Department of Commerce to formalise a review process for frontier AI. The agreements are drafted to be flexible enough to accommodate the rapid pace of model development and include classified-environment testing capability.

This is the broadest formal pre-deployment oversight regime the US government has established to date, effectively making government safety vetting a standard expectation for frontier AI releases from the major labs.
